Attraction Features

Kuromon Ichiba Market is located in the southern area of Nippombashi, near the center of Osaka city. Within a 580-meter arcade, there are approximately 170 shops including fishmongers, greengrocers, butchers, and snack shops. Known as the kitchen that supports the daily meals of Osaka residents, the market attracts around 30,000 visitors per day on regular days and up to 150,000 visitors during the year-end period. It is believed that fish trading started here as early as 1822 in the Edo period, and it was initially called Enmeiji Market, named after the nearby Enmeiji Temple. The market later came to be known as Kuromon Ichiba Market because the gate of Enmeiji Temple was black in color. Originally renowned as a market frequented by professional chefs, the market has seen an increase in general customers in recent years, with many tourists enjoying the pleasure of walking and eating.

  • Trusted fishmongers selling high-quality seafood: It is said that fishmongers have gathered at Kuromon Ichiba Market since the Edo period to buy and sell fresh fish. As a market specializing in seafood, it has prospered throughout history, and thus, there are still many fish shops today. Eel in summer and pufferfish in winter are local specialties, with 10% of the fish shops selling pufferfish. Renowned chefs from upscale Japanese restaurants also come to this market to procure fresh and high-quality fish.
  • paradise where you can walk and eat with the determination to eat until you drop: Not only for professionals but also for general customers, Kuromon Ichiba Market opens its doors wide. Various mouthwatering fresh foods are displayed at the shop entrances, and energetic staff members loudly welcome customers, saying, "Welcome!" Many shops sell their products skewered on bamboo sticks for customers to enjoy while walking, and there are also seating areas available for dining in.
  • tourist center that provides comprehensive services to foreign visitors: Opened in 2016, the "Kuromon Tourist Center" offers many delightful services to the increasing number of foreign tourists in recent years. The center has English- and Chinese-speaking staff members on duty and provides brochures in various languages. They also have currency exchange machines, so visitors can use them even if they don't have enough Japanese yen. Additionally, they offer services such as delivery to hotels and baggage storage.

Details

It takes an 8-minute walk to reach MyStays Puremia Hotel (double room, 1486/person). You can leave your luggage there and then walk for another 10 minutes to reach Higashi-Umeda Subway Station. Take the Tanimachi Line to Temmabashi Rokuchome, and it's a 3-minute walk to Osaka Museum of History (using the Osaka Amazing Pass).
From Temmabashi Rokuchome, take the Tanimachi Line to Tanimachi Yonchome, and it's a 16-minute walk to visit Osaka Castle Keep (using the Osaka Amazing Pass). From Tanimachi Yonchome, take the Chuo Line to Sakaisuji Hommachi and transfer to the Sakaisuji Line to Ebisucho. Walk for 2 minutes to reach Tsutenkaku (using the Osaka Amazing Pass).
From Ebisucho, take the Sakaisuji Line to Nipponbashi Station and transfer to the Sen-Nichimae Line to Namba Station. It's an 8-minute walk to Kuromon Ichiba Market and a 2-minute walk to Dotonbori, where you can experience the Dotonbori River Cruise (using the Osaka Amazing Pass).
From Namba, take the Midosuji Line to Shinsaibashi. From Shinsaibashi, take the Chuo Line to Nagahoribashi and transfer to the Sakaisuji Line to Temmabashi Rokuchome, where you can explore Tenjinbashisuji Shopping Street.
From Temmabashi Rokuchome, take the Tanimachi Line to Higashi-Umeda, and it's a 16-minute walk to Umeda Sky Building (using the Osaka Amazing Pass for the Ferris wheel).
